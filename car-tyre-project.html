Section 01

Product Description

A rubber component mounted on the wheel rim that ensures contact between the vehicle and the road, providing mobility, grip, stability, and safety under different driving conditions.

Functional Unit

The provision of safe road mobility for a 1,500 kg standard passenger vehicle over 40,000 km under normal driving conditions in Italy, using one set of four basic all-season tyres, over a reference period of 4 years.

Reference distance40,000 km
Avg. time to cover distance4 years
Avg. trip length9.2 km
Main trip purposeWork-related trips
Vehicle typeStandard passenger car — 1,500 kg
Tyre typeAll-season, one set of four
Geographic referenceItaly, 2021–2025

Main Components

Main Body

The bulk of the tyre made of rubber compounds. Provides structure and allows the tyre to flex while resisting wear.

Innerliner

A thin, airtight rubber layer on the inside of the tyre. Keeps air pressure stable over time.

Reinforcements

The structural "skeleton" made of steel cord & textile fibres. Provides stiffness, shape, and improves stability and puncture resistance.

Curing & Key Chemicals

Small-added ingredients used during manufacturing to enable vulcanization (crosslinking) and control the final properties.

⚠️

Nylon-6 (Critical)

Highest SIE (6.13×10⁴). Very high carbon footprint (7.69 kg CO₂/kg), high water usage (265 l/kg), and 0% recycled content.

💧

Natural Rubber (Water)

Extremely high water usage: 17,321 l/kg — driven by the agricultural nature of rubber tree cultivation.

🏭

Butyl Rubber IIR

Highest embodied energy (92.07 MJ/kg) and highest carbon footprint (4.314 kg CO₂/kg) among all rubber compounds.

♻️

AISI 1080 Steel (Best)

Best recyclability at 51.94% recycled fraction. Lowest embodied energy (18.97 MJ/kg). Most circular material in the tyre.

Section 06

Possible Solutions

♻️ Solution 1 — Retread / Remanufacturing of the Casing

Reusing the casing of the tyre and replacing only the worn part (mainly the tread). Retread uses less than one third of the raw materials needed for a new tyre.

Technical

Does not require redesigning the tyre from scratch. Already established industrial technology, especially in the truck and fleet sector.

Economic

Currently the most cost-effective solution. Avoids remanufacturing expensive materials such as Carbon Black, Silica, Nylon-6, and ZnO.

Environmental

Reduces both the use of virgin raw materials and the amount of tyre waste generated.

Critical Issues

Not all casings can be reused. An organized collection and inspection chain is required. Passenger car market perception is still weaker.

🖤 Solution 2 — Partial Replacement of Carbon Black with rCB

Specific recycling of Carbon (industrial) — not reuse of the same tyre. rCB (recovered Carbon Black) is recovered from end-of-life tyres through pyrolysis or thermolysis.

Technical

Does not require a complete redesign. Allows partial replacement of Carbon Black in selected compounds when formulation is properly optimized.

Economic

Partial replacement can reduce costs, although actual benefit depends on quality, purity, post-treatments, and industrial scale.

Environmental

Reduces the use of virgin Carbon Black, increases material circularity, and upgrades end-of-life tyres into a secondary raw material.

Critical Issues

Main limits are quality variability, presence of ash/mineral residues, and reinforcing performance often lower than virgin material.
